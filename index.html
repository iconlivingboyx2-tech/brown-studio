Philosophy

Brown is stability. Structure is form.

We reduce the coat to its essential elements. No excess. No decoration beyond function. Each piece in the archive is defined by three principles: structure, restraint, and form.

The brown palette—ranging from camel to espresso—provides the foundation. It is the color of earth, of archive, of permanence. In a world of noise, we choose the stability of restraint.
